Hi, I'm Julius. I am a polar climate scientist and ice sheet modeler in the Earth System Analysis department at the Potsdam Institute for Climate Impact Research (PIK), Germany. Currently, I am also a PhD candidate at the University of Potsdam.

My main research interests focus on the long-term stability behavior and tipping points of the Antarctic Ice Sheet and their impact on global sea level under future climate warming.
Furthermore, I am interested in how climate and socio-economic tipping processes influence and interact with each other in the Earth system.

I am also actively contributing to the development of the Parallel Ice Sheet Model (PISM), an open-source computer model for the simulation of ice sheets and glaciers.

At PIK, I am part of the working group on Ice Dynamics led by Prof. Ricarda Winkelmann as well as PIK's FutureLab on Earth Resilience in the Anthropocene. My work is funded by the Leibniz project DominoES on interacting tipping elements in the Earth system and the EU's Horizon 2020 project TiPACCs on tipping points in Antarctic climate components.
